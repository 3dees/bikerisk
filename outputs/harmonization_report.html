Regulatory Intent

To ensure that lithium-ion secondary batteries used in e-bikes and light electric vehicles are designed, manufactured, and classified according to recognized safety standards and appropriate UN transport classifications for safe handling, storage, and shipment.

Consolidated Requirement

Lithium-ion secondary batteries and battery systems shall comply with the following safety and classification requirements:

a) Battery Safety Standard Compliance: Lithium-ion secondary batteries shall meet the safety requirements specified in IEC 62133-2 or equivalent recognized standards for lithium-ion secondary battery safety.

b) UN Transport Classification: Lithium-ion batteries and battery-equipped vehicles shall be classified and handled according to the appropriate United Nations transport classification as follows:
1) Lithium-ion batteries (standalone): UN 3480
2) Lithium-ion batteries contained in equipment: UN 3481
3) Lithium-ion batteries packed with equipment: UN 3481
4) Battery-powered vehicle or battery-powered equipment (including light electric vehicles): UN 3171
5) Hybrid electric vehicle (HEV): UN 3166

c) Light Electric Vehicle Classification: For light electric vehicles (e-bikes, e-scooters, etc.), the UN 3171 classification for "Battery-powered vehicle or battery powered equipment" applies and shall be used for transport documentation and handling procedures.

Regulatory Intent

To ensure that e-bikes and their battery systems are tested under defined environmental conditions, including temperature extremes and severe operating conditions, to verify safe performance across the expected range of use and storage scenarios.

Consolidated Requirement

The e-bike and battery system shall be tested under specified environmental conditions to verify safe performance. Testing shall include:
a) Temperature conditions as specified in the applicable test procedures, including ambient temperature ranges for normal operation, storage, and charging
b) Severe conditions testing, which may include but is not limited to: extreme temperatures (high and low), humidity extremes, mechanical stress, abnormal charging/discharging conditions, and other foreseeable adverse environmental or operational scenarios
c) All testing shall be conducted in accordance with the test methods and acceptance criteria defined in the applicable safety standard(s)

Regulatory Intent

To ensure that battery systems and devices under test (DUT) do not exhibit catastrophic failure modes during and after safety testing that could pose fire, explosion, or hazardous material release risks to users or the environment.

Consolidated Requirement

During all safety tests and throughout the specified post-test observation period, the device under test (DUT) shall meet the following acceptance criteria:

a) Fire and Explosion: The DUT shall exhibit no evidence of fire or explosion at any time during the test or observation period.

b) Leakage: The DUT shall exhibit no evidence of leakage of electrolyte, gases, or other hazardous materials.

c) Physical Integrity: The DUT shall exhibit no evidence of:
i) Rupture of the DUT enclosure or battery cells
ii) Disassembly of components or structural elements

d) Electrical Isolation (for voltage class B DUT only): Voltage class B DUT shall maintain an isolation resistance of at least 100 Ω/V throughout the test and observation period.

e) Observation Period Duration: The post-test observation period shall be:
i) 1 hour minimum for standard tests
ii) 6 hours for tests specifically evaluating leakage, rupture, fire, or explosion risks

f) Installation Orientation (when applicable): For tests requiring specific orientation, the DUT shall be placed within the test chamber in the manner in which it will be installed in the vehicle.

g) Continuous Charge Testing: During continuous charge at constant voltage, the DUT shall not exhibit leakage, fire, or explosion.

Regulatory Intent

To ensure consistent and traceable test execution by specifying sample preparation procedures, monitoring parameters for informational purposes during testing, observation periods, and acceptance criteria that prevent fire propagation or explosion.

Consolidated Requirement

Test sample preparation, monitoring, and observation shall comply with the following requirements:

a) Sample Preparation and Mounting:
1) Samples shall be prepared according to the specific test requirements, with additional preparation details provided where applicable for clarity
2) For mechanical tests requiring fixturing, the fully charged sample shall be secured to the testing machine by means of a rigid mount that supports all mounting surfaces of the sample
3) For cleaning tests, the sample shall be rubbed for 15 seconds with a piece of cloth soaked with petroleum spirit

b) State of Charge:
1) Tests shall be conducted on fully charged devices under test (DUT) at manufacturer's specified maximum state of charge (MOSOC)
2) The fully charged condition shall be maintained unless otherwise specified by the particular test procedure

c) Test Execution Parameters:
1) For thermal exposure tests, the sample shall be placed in a full-draft circulating-air oven maintained at a uniform temperature of 70°C (158°F), with samples remaining in the oven for 7 hours
2) For rotation tests, the fully charged sample shall be rotated at a continuous rate of 90°/15 seconds, conducting testing by subjecting the sample to a 360° rotation in 3 mutually perpendicular different directions; devices with only 2 axes of symmetry (such as cylindrical designs) shall be subjected to 2 mutually perpendicular directions of rotation
3) For crush tests, the sample shall be crushed between a fixed surface and a ribbed test platen having semi-cylindrical intruders with a 75-mm (2.95-in) radius placed 30-mm (1.2-in) from one another across the face of the platen
4) For impact tests, a 15.8 mm diameter bar shall be placed across the test sample cell or component cell, then a 9.1 kg mass shall be dropped from a height of 61 cm onto the bar
5) For tests requiring concrete surfaces, the concrete surface shall be at least 76-mm (3-in) thick and shall be large enough in area to cover the DUT

d) Monitoring and Observation:
1) During testing, the open circuit voltage (OCV) of the sample and temperatures on the center cell(s/modules) may be monitored for information purposes
2) Temperature monitoring on the center cell shall be conducted for information purposes where applicable
3) For impact and similar tests, samples shall be observed for 6 hours following the test event
4) Monitoring for information purposes should be conducted during rotation testing

e) Temperature Measurement Accuracy:
1) Temperature measurements shall maintain accuracy of ±2°C (±3.6°F) for temperatures at or below 200°C (392°F)
2) Temperature measurements shall maintain accuracy of ±3% for temperatures above 200°C (392°F)

f) Fire Propagation Test Setup:
1) Cheesecloth (bleached cotton cloth of approximately 40 g/m²) shall be placed 100 mm distance above the DUT
2) After the test, this cheesecloth shall remain intact

g) Acceptance Criteria:
1) As a result of testing, there shall be no fire propagating from the DUT or explosion of the DUT
2) The deterioration of the devices under evaluation shall not result in unacceptable properties (i.e., reduced performance that would result in malfunction of the connection)
3) The deterioration shall not be greater than that of the comparison design where applicable

h) Documentation and Guidance:
1) Conformity can be checked by examination of manufacturer's documentation
2) Guidance on design and manufacture shall be referenced from applicable technical reports and informative annexes

Regulatory Intent

These clauses collectively specify the precise dimensional, material, and procedural requirements for test probes and nickel particles used in safety testing. The test finger probe is used for accessibility testing to ensure hazardous parts are not reachable, while the nickel particle specifications enable controlled internal short circuit testing of lithium-ion cells to verify their safety response under fault conditions.

Consolidated Requirement

Test equipment and materials for safety testing shall conform to the following specifications:

a) Test Finger Probe (for accessibility testing):
1) Dimensions: As specified in EN 61032:1998, Figure 2, test probe B, but without joints
2) Material: Metal, except where otherwise specified
3) Tolerances (for dimensions without specified tolerances):
- Angles: +0° / -10°
- Linear dimensions ≤25 mm: +0 mm / -0.05 mm
- Linear dimensions >25 mm: ±0.2 mm
4) All linear dimensions are in millimetres

b) Nickel Particle (for internal short circuit testing):
1) Shape: L-shaped as shown in applicable standard figures
2) Dimensions:
- Height: 0.2 mm
- Thickness: 0.1 mm
- L-shape angle: 90° ±10°
- Each side of L-shape: 1.0 mm with 5% tolerance
3) Material: More than 99% pure nickel (mass fraction)

c) Nickel Particle Insertion Procedure for Cylindrical Cells:
1) Insertion between positive active material zone and negative active material zone:
i) IF the outer turn of the positive substrate is aluminum foil, cut the foil at the separation line between the aluminum foil and active material to perform short circuit test between positive and negative active materials
ii) Insert the nickel particle between the positive active material and the separator
iii) Alignment: As shown in applicable standard figures
iv) Position: 20 mm from the edge of the aluminum foil cut
v) Direction: L-shaped corner in the winding direction
2) Insertion between positive aluminum foil (uncoated zone) and negative coated zone (active material):
i) WHEN positive electrode aluminum foil is exposed on the outer turn and faces the negative active material coating, use the following procedure
ii) WHEN positive electrode aluminum foil is exposed on the outer turn, cut the aluminum foil 10 mm from the separation line between the aluminum foil and active material
iii) Insert the nickel particle between the aluminum foil and the separator
iv) Alignment: As shown in applicable standard figures
v) Position: 1.0 mm from the edge of the positive active material coating on the aluminum foil
3) Mark the position of the nickel particle on both ends of the wound core separator using the following procedure:
i) Place insulating sheet between the separator facing the nickel particle and the negative electrode to protect against short circuits
ii) Manually wind the electrodes and separator while holding the nickel particle in place and apply adhesive tape to the wound core
iii) Mark the position of the nickel particle on the wound core
iv) Place the wound core in a zip-lock polyethylene bag and seal it tightly
v) Place the polyethylene bag in an aluminum laminated bag to prevent drying
vi) Complete the procedure within 30 minutes

d) Nickel Particle Insertion Procedure for Prismatic Cells:
1) Before inserting the nickel particle, place an insulating sheet between the negative electrode and the separator located below the nickel particle and the negative electrode to protect against short circuits
2) Insertion between positive coated zone (active material) and negative coated zone (active material):
i) Insert the nickel particle between the positive coated zone (active material) and the separator OR between the separator and the negative coated zone (active material)
ii) FOR aluminum cell cases, insert the nickel particle between the positive coated zone (active material) and the separator
iii) Insert the nickel particle between the positive active material and the separator
iv) Alignment: As shown in applicable standard figures
v) Position: Center (diagonally) of the wound core
vi) Direction: L-shaped corner of the nickel particle in the winding direction

e) Nickel Particle Positioning Recommendations:
1) WHEN the nickel particle cannot be placed in the position described above, the position may be modified
2) FOR prismatic cells, a nickel particle may be placed on a flat surface, but it shall be positioned at the center of the surface under pressure
3) IF it is difficult to place a nickel particle under the outermost layer, it may be placed under an inner layer as shown in applicable standard figures
4) A nickel particle shall NOT be placed in a zone where the positive active material has detached from the aluminum foil
5) IF material has detached in the specified zone, place the nickel particle in another zone where positive active material exists and where it can be compressed (with the center of the pressure clamping jig)
6) The position of the nickel particle may be determined by the cell manufacturer and the test technician

f) Test Preparation and Packaging:
1) Disassemble the charged cell as specified in applicable procedures
2) Place one wound core in one zip-lock polyethylene bag, then place in one aluminum laminated bag
3) To minimize electrolyte vaporization, use bags as small as possible (example: polyethylene bag 100 mm width × 140 mm height × 0.04 mm thickness; aluminum laminated bag 120 mm width × 180 mm height × 0.11 mm thickness)
4) Set oven temperature as specified in applicable test requirements
5) Place the wound core and nickel particle in an aluminum foil bag and place in the oven for 45 minutes ±15 minutes
6) Remove the wound core from the sealed bag and attach voltage measurement leads and thermocouple for temperature to the surface of the wound core
7) Place the wound core in the mounting jig to locate the position of the nickel particle under the press
8) Remove the insulating sheet and close the oven door

Regulatory Intent

These clauses collectively establish standardized procedures for preparing and inserting nickel particles into lithium-ion cells to deliberately induce internal short circuits during safety testing. This ensures consistent, reproducible test conditions for evaluating cell safety performance under internal short circuit conditions.

Consolidated Requirement

For internal short circuit testing of lithium-ion cells, nickel particles shall be prepared and inserted according to the following requirements:

a) Nickel Particle Shape and Dimensions:
- The shape of the nickel particle shall conform to the standardized L-shaped configuration as specified in the applicable test standard figures
- Dimensions shall match the completed nickel particle specifications provided in the standard

b) Material and Tools:
- Use materials and tools as specified in the preparation procedure
- Follow the example nickel particle preparation procedure provided in the standard

c) Insertion Procedure for Cylindrical Cells:
- Between positive and negative active material coated areas:
i) Insert nickel particle between positive active material and separator
ii) Align the nickel particle as shown in the standard figures
iii) Position the insertion of the nickel particle 20 mm from the edge of the cut aluminium foil
iv) Direct the L-shaped corner towards the direction of winding
- Between positive aluminium foil (uncoated area) and negative active material coated area:
i) When aluminium foil of positive electrode is exposed at outer turn and facing the coated negative active material, cut off the aluminium foil 10 mm from the dividing line between aluminium foil and active material
ii) Insert nickel particle between aluminium foil and separator

iv) Position the insertion of nickel particle at 1.0 mm from the edge of the coating of positive active material on aluminium foil

e) Positioning and Placement:
- Position the nickel particle according to the specified placement procedures
- IF the nickel particle cannot be placed in the specified area, follow the alternative positioning guidance provided in the standard

f) Quality Control:
- Test samples should have passed the quality control procedure of the supplier
- The supplier should provide a quality examination report

g) Cell Disassembly:
- Follow the disassembly procedures specified for cylindrical and prismatic cells as applicable
