Regulatory Intent

To ensure comprehensive safety across battery and e-bike systems through proper testing procedures, protective equipment requirements, user and manufacturer guidance, and safe handling protocols. This includes temperature management, mechanical testing specifications, safety information dissemination, and protective measures during testing and normal use.

Consolidated Requirement

The following miscellaneous safety requirements shall be implemented:

a) Temperature, Voltage, and Current Management: Systems shall incorporate temperature, voltage, and current management controls as specified in design requirements.

b) Testing Temperature Protocols:
1) When conducting tests at elevated temperatures, pressurization shall commence within 3 minutes after placing the wound core on the testing machine to minimize electrolyte vaporization
2) When conducting tests at low temperatures, testing shall commence within 10 minutes
3) Pressurization shall begin only after the wound core reaches the specified testing temperature

c) Mechanical Testing Equipment Specifications:
1) Pressing devices shall conform to recommended specifications including servo-motor or hydraulic press configurations
2) Servo-motor presses are recommended over hydraulic presses due to immediate stopping capability
3) Equipment shall follow specified distance/time ratios and jig configurations as detailed in technical drawings

d) Test-Specific Requirements:
1) Mechanical stability tests for thermoplastic housings exposed to sunlight shall be performed without cells in the housing, with internal temperature recording
2) Touch current tests shall verify switch-off device functionality where accessible conductive parts or contacts exist
3) Over-temperature and under-temperature condition tests shall be conducted per specified protocols
4) Thermal abuse tests shall verify that external cell temperature does not exceed 170°C and that no disassembly or fire occurs within 6 hours

e) Cell Assembly and Protection:
1) Cells shall be assembled into batteries with appropriate mechanical protection for cells and components
2) Multi-cell battery cases shall be designed to open only with the aid of a tool
3) Equipment shall prohibit incorrect insertion of cells or batteries and shall have clear polarity marks

f) Internal Short Circuit Test Preparation (when applicable):
1) Nickel particles shall be prepared according to specified shape, dimensions, and material requirements
2) For cylindrical cells: Insert nickel particle between positive and negative active material coated areas, or between positive aluminium foil (uncoated area) and negative coated area, at specified positions (20mm from edge or 1.0mm from coating edge as applicable)
3) For prismatic cells: Insert nickel particle between positive active material and separator, 20mm from edge of cut aluminium foil, with L-shaped corner towards winding direction
4) Particle alignment and positioning shall follow specified figures and dimensional requirements
5) Cell disassembly procedures shall follow documented methods for cylindrical and prismatic cell types

g) Safety Equipment and Work Area Requirements:
1) Personnel shall wear long-sleeved protective clothing, protective glasses, mask, and gloves during testing
2) Unnecessary flammable materials shall not be placed in work areas
3) Fire protection cloth or sand shall be available, and gas exhaust systems shall be effective
4) Countermeasures shall prevent cell contents from scattering when cells catch fire

h) Quality Control and Documentation:
1) Test samples shall pass supplier quality control procedures
2) Suppliers shall provide quality examination reports
3) Suppliers shall provide cycling data, intended application information, and additional information needed by test laboratories
4) Original cell and battery literature shall be retained for future reference

i) Safety Information Requirements:
1) Manufacturers shall provide guidance on design and manufacture per applicable technical reports
2) Safety information shall be provided for small cells and batteries
3) Information shall include recommendations to equipment manufacturers, battery assemblers, and end-users

j) User and Manufacturer Recommendations:
1) DO NOT dismantle, open, or shred cells or batteries (dismantling only by trained personnel)
2) DO NOT expose cells or batteries to heat, fire, or direct sunlight storage
3) DO NOT mix cells of different manufacture, capacity, size, or type within a battery or device
4) DO NOT use cells or batteries not designed for the specific equipment
5) Observe polarity marks (+ and −) on cells, batteries, and equipment
6) Purchase only manufacturer-recommended batteries for equipment
7) Wipe terminals with clean dry cloth if dirty
8) Remove battery from equipment when not in use (when possible)
9) Do not leave battery on prolonged charge when not in use
10) After extended storage, charge and discharge cycles may be necessary for maximum performance
11) Use cells or batteries only in intended applications
12) Seek immediate medical advice if cell or battery is swallowed
13) Consult manufacturer on maximum cell assembly numbers and safest connection methods
14) Keep cells or batteries of different electrochemical systems separate during disposal

k) Intended Use and Application: All testing and use shall conform to intended use specifications and application requirements as documented.

Regulatory Intent

To establish clear pass/fail criteria for safety testing of battery systems and e-bikes, ensuring no catastrophic failures occur during or after testing, and to ensure proper documentation and traceability of test samples and component certifications.

Consolidated Requirement

Battery systems and e-bikes under test (DUT) shall meet the following acceptance criteria and documentation requirements:

a) Test Acceptance Criteria - During testing and applicable post-test observation periods, the DUT shall exhibit no evidence of:
1) Leakage of electrolyte or other fluids
2) Enclosure rupture or disassembly
3) Fire or explosion
4) For voltage class B DUT: loss of isolation resistance below 100 Ω/V (measured during test and 1 hour post-test observation period)

b) Post-Test Observation Periods:
1) Standard observation: 1 hour post-test for fire, explosion, and isolation resistance verification
2) Extended observation: 6 hours post-test for leakage, rupture, fire, or explosion assessment (where specified by test protocol)

c) Fire Test Specific Criteria:
1) Cheesecloth (bleached cotton cloth of approximately 40 g/m²) shall be placed 100 mm above the DUT during fire testing
2) After completion of fire test, the cheesecloth shall remain intact with no evidence of ignition or damage

d) Documentation Requirements - The following documentation shall be delivered with the DUT:
1) Certificates for all components and subsystems (refer to applicable standard's component certificate table for specific requirements)
2) Overview of all provided test samples including:
- Serial numbers for each sample
- Cycle history for each sample

e) Measurement Procedures:
1) Internal AC resistance measurements shall be performed according to the procedures specified in the applicable test standard

Differences Across Standards

Standard Clauses Specific Differences / Stricter Requirements
IEC 62133-2 D.2.b.3 Specifically addresses internal AC resistance measurement procedures as a distinct requirement, whereas EN 50604-1 does not explicitly call out this measurement methodology.
EN 50604-1 6.102.3, 6.103.3, 8.3.103 Provides varying post-test observation periods (1 hour vs 6 hours) depending on the specific test being performed. Also uniquely specifies isolation resistance threshold of 100 Ω/V for voltage class B DUT.
EN 50604-1 5.109 Uniquely specifies cheesecloth fire test criteria with specific material specification (40 g/m² bleached cotton) and placement distance (100 mm), derived from IEC 62368-1:2010.
EN 50604-1 EE.2.4, EE.2.6 Requires comprehensive documentation package including component certificates (with reference to detailed table) and sample tracking information (serial numbers and cycle history), which is not explicitly stated in IEC 62133-2 clauses in this cluster.

Unique Requirements

EN 50604-1 clause 10.1.103 and 8.101.3 specifically prohibit 'disassembly' as a distinct failure mode separate from rupture, providing additional granularity in failure classification. IEC 62133-2 D.2.b.3 uniquely addresses the procedural requirement for AC resistance measurement methodology.

Regulatory Intent

To define precise specifications for test equipment and materials used in safety testing procedures, including test finger probes for accessibility testing and nickel particle specifications for internal short circuit testing of battery cells, ensuring reproducible and standardized test conditions across laboratories.

Consolidated Requirement

Test equipment and materials for safety testing shall conform to the following specifications:

a) Test Finger Probe (for accessibility testing):
1) The test finger shall be derived from EN 61032:1998, Figure 2, test probe B, without joints
2) Material: metal, except where otherwise specified
3) Tolerances on dimensions without specified tolerances:
- On angles: +0° / −10°
- On linear dimensions ≤ 25 mm: +0 mm / −0.05 mm
- On linear dimensions > 25 mm: ±0.2 mm
4) All linear dimensions are in millimetres
5) Refer to applicable standard figure for complete dimensional specifications

b) Nickel Particle (for internal short circuit testing of battery cells):
1) Shape: L-shaped as specified in applicable standard figures
2) Dimensions:
- Height: 0.2 mm
- Thickness: 0.1 mm
- L shape angle: 90° ± 10°
- Each side of L: 1.0 mm with 5% tolerance
3) Material: more than 99% (mass fraction) pure nickel

c) Nickel Particle Insertion Procedures for Cylindrical Cells:
1) Insertion between positive active material zone and negative active material zone:
i) IF the outer turn of the positive substrate is aluminum foil, cut the foil at the separation line between the aluminum foil and active material to perform short circuit test between positive and negative active materials
ii) Insert the nickel particle between the positive active material and the separator at 20 mm from the edge of the aluminum foil cut
iii) The L-shaped corner direction shall be in the winding direction
iv) Alignment of the nickel particle shall be as shown in applicable standard figures
2) Insertion between positive aluminum foil (uncoated zone) and negative coated zone (active material):
i) WHEN the positive electrode aluminum foil is exposed on the outer turn and the aluminum foil faces the coated negative active material, use the following procedure
ii) WHEN the positive electrode aluminum foil is exposed on the outer turn, cut the aluminum foil at 10 mm from the separation line between the aluminum foil and active material
iii) Insert the nickel particle between the aluminum foil and the separator at 1.0 mm from the edge of the positive active material coating on the aluminum foil
iv) Alignment of the nickel particle shall be as shown in applicable standard figures
3) General procedure:
i) Mark the position of the nickel particle on both ends of the separator winding
ii) Place insulating sheet between the separator facing the nickel particle and the negative electrode to protect against short circuits
iii) Manually wind the electrodes and separator while maintaining the nickel particle in place and apply adhesive tape to the winding
iv) Mark the position of the nickel particle on the winding
v) Place the winding in a polyethylene bag with tight seal and close hermetically
vi) Place the polyethylene bag in an aluminum-coated bag to prevent drying
vii) Complete the procedure within 30 minutes

d) Nickel Particle Insertion Procedures for Prismatic Cells:
1) Before inserting the nickel particle, place an insulating sheet between the negative electrode and the separator located below the nickel particle and the negative electrode to protect against short circuits
2) Insertion of nickel particle in the winding:
i) Insert the nickel particle between the positive coated zone (active material) and the separator OR between the separator and the negative coated zone (active material)
ii) IN CASE OF aluminum cell case, insert the nickel particle between the positive coated zone (active material) and the separator
iii) Insert the nickel particle between the positive active material and the separator
iv) Alignment of the nickel particle shall be as shown in applicable standard figures
v) The nickel particle is positioned at the center (diagonally) of the winding
vi) The L-shaped corner direction of the nickel particle is in the winding direction

e) Test Preparation with Nickel Particle:
1) Set oven temperature as specified in applicable test table
2) Place the winding and nickel particle in an aluminum foil bag and place in oven for 45 minutes ± 15 minutes
3) Remove the winding from the sealed bag and attach voltage measurement leads and thermocouple for temperature to the winding surface
4) Place the winding in the mounting fixture to locate the nickel particle position under the press
5) Remove the insulating sheet and close the oven door

f) Explosion Test Result Criteria:
Rupturing is considered to have occurred WHERE solid parts of the device under test have penetrated a wire mesh screen placed 25 cm away from the device under test, with screen specifications:
- Material: annealed aluminum wire
- Wire diameter: 0.25 mm
- Grid density: 6 to 7 wires per cm

Regulatory Intent

To ensure that cells and batteries can withstand external short circuit conditions without fire, explosion, or rupture, thereby protecting users and equipment from catastrophic failure during accidental short circuit events.

Consolidated Requirement

Cells and batteries shall be tested for external short circuit resistance to verify safe behavior under fault conditions. The following requirements apply:

a) Cell-level external short circuit test:
i) Test each cell type individually for external short circuit conditions
ii) Apply test conditions as specified in IEC 62133-2 clause 7.3.1
iii) Cells shall not catch fire or explode during or after the test

b) Battery-level external short circuit test:
i) Test complete battery assemblies for external short circuit conditions
ii) Apply test conditions as specified in IEC 62133-2 clause 7.3.2
iii) Batteries shall not catch fire or explode during or after the test

c) Short circuit test procedure (applicable to both cells and batteries):
i) Stabilize the test sample at 55 ± 2 °C ambient temperature
ii) Apply an external resistance of less than 0.1 Ω across the terminals
iii) Maintain the short circuit condition for 1 hour
iv) After removing the short circuit, observe the sample for an additional 6 hours
v) During the entire test duration (short circuit period plus observation period), the sample shall not exhibit fire, explosion, or rupture

d) Pass criteria:
i) No fire occurrence
ii) No explosion
iii) No rupture or venting that could lead to safety hazards
iv) Sample may exhibit temperature rise, swelling, or leakage, but must not create immediate safety hazards

Regulatory Intent

To ensure that battery management systems and protection circuits prevent fire, explosion, or excessive temperature rise when the battery terminals are externally short-circuited, either through inherent design or active protection mechanisms that disconnect the short-circuit current.

Consolidated Requirement

The battery and its protection system shall prevent fire or explosion during external short-circuit conditions and comply with the following:
a) Protection requirement: The battery management system or overcurrent protection function shall disconnect the short-circuit current when an external short-circuit occurs.
b) Test conditions for batteries without active protection or with single-fault simulation: A fully charged battery (charged according to manufacturer's standard charging procedure) shall be stored at ambient temperature of 20 °C ± 5 °C, then short-circuited by connecting the positive and negative terminals with a total external resistance of 80 mΩ ± 20 mΩ.
c) Test duration: The battery shall remain on test for 24 hours OR until the case temperature of the battery declines by 20% of the maximum temperature rise, whichever occurs sooner. However, in case of rapid decline in short-circuit current, the battery shall remain on test for an additional one hour after the current reaches a low-end steady state condition.
d) Single fault condition testing: For batteries with discharge protection circuits, a single fault in the discharge protection circuit shall be conducted on one to four samples (depending upon the protection circuit configuration) before conducting the short-circuit test.
e) Acceptance criteria: The device under test shall exhibit:
i) No fire
ii) No explosion
iii) No disassembly
iv) Battery casing temperature shall not exceed 150 °C within 6 hours of the short-circuit event.

Regulatory Intent

To establish standardized definitions for expressing battery current measurements and test parameters, ensuring consistent interpretation of charge/discharge current values and enabling reproducible test conditions based on rated capacity.

Consolidated Requirement

Battery current measurements and test parameters shall be defined and expressed as follows:

a) Reference test current (I): The charge or discharge current shall be expressed as a multiple of IA, where IA = CAh/1 h (as defined in IEC 61434), based on the rated capacity (CAh) of the cell or battery. This reference current provides the basis for specifying test conditions relative to the battery's rated capacity.

b) Sign convention for battery current: The sign of battery current shall be specified such that:
1) Discharge current is designated as positive (+)
2) Charge current is designated as negative (-)

All technical documentation, test specifications, and user manuals shall apply these definitions consistently when referencing battery current values and test parameters.
